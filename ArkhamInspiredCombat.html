Project

This project recreates the iconic free-flow combat system from the Batman Arkham series inside Unreal Engine 5, built entirely in C++. My goal was to capture the cinematic feel of Rocksteady’s combat — fluid chaining between enemies, responsive counters, and AI that engages the player without overwhelming them. The system integrates smart enemy targeting, root-motion gap-closing, animation-driven hit windows, and group attack coordination, all while respecting player control and camera freedom. It serves as both a technical showcase and a gameplay experiment in translating AAA mechanics into a custom system from scratch.

My Work

As the sole developer, I designed and programmed the entire combat framework from the ground up in C++. I implemented a dynamic targeting and free-flow chaining system that automatically selects the most appropriate enemy and rotates the player into seamless follow-up attacks. Combat is fully animation-driven, with montages and notifies controlling hit windows, weapon collisions, damage values, and polished feedback such as hit-pause and camera shake to emphasize impact. I also built a counter and parry system, where enemy telegraphs open short input windows that branch into specialized counter animations, stuns, or disarms. On the AI side, I extended Behavior Trees with custom combat states and a lightweight group manager that coordinates enemy behavior—only allowing certain enemies to attack while others circle, feint, or recover—ensuring encounters remain cinematic and readable rather than chaotic.
